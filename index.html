      Our primary focus has been on developing a cutting-edge Natural Language Processing (NLP) algorithm specifically tailored for Ancient Greek and Latin languages. Leveraging an ALBERT model, our algorithm excels in aspect extraction and sentiment analysis (ABSA) tasks, contributing to the advancement of computational humanities.

    6. Undergraduate Researcher - Software Developer

      Sep 2021 - Aug 2022

      Before becoming a team lead in the Data Driven Humanities Research Group, I was a software developer on the team. In my role as a researcher, I aided the development of a Natural Language Processing (NLP) deep learning algorithm using the SKLearn Python module to investigate the historical roots of racial, occupational, and sexual prejudices in the classical world. This involved not only conceptualizing and implementing the algorithm but also managing and processing an extensive dataset.
      To do so, I scraped and parsed over 1000 XML files with varying schemas, consolidating them into a unified CSV format for streamlined data processing. To optimize the efficiency of algorithms and manage output data complexity, I employed sparse matrices, maps, trees, and dataframes using numPy and pandas.
      Collaboration was a cornerstone of this project, and I seamlessly integrated input from fellow researchers spanning diverse fields such as computer science, mathematics, and humanities. This experience not only showcased my technical proficiency but also highlighted my ability to bridge interdisciplinary gaps and contribute to a multifaceted research endeavor.
